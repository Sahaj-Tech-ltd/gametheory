## Core Geopolitical Agents

### United States — Hegemonic Defender

| Attribute | Value |
|:----------|:------|
| **Personality** | Hegemonic Defender |
| **Region** | North America |
| **Resources** | 95 |
| **Military** | 95 |
| **Influence** | 95 |
| **Nuclear** | Yes |

**Strategy**: The US plays containment — squeeze rivals economically, surround them with bases, wait for them to collapse from within. Uses tit-for-tat on trade (matching tariffs and sanctions blow for blow). Nuclear posture as deterrent, never first use.

**Allies**: Israel, Ukraine, Taiwan, Saudi Arabia (fragile), India (strategic partner)

**Rivals**: Russia, China, Iran

**Dependencies (vulnerabilities)**:
- Semiconductors from Taiwan
- Oil from Saudi Arabia
- Rare earths from China (critical vulnerability)

**Core Tension**: Overextension. Every alliance is a liability, every deployment drains resources. The US talks about freedom but operates on cold realpolitik.

## Agent Design Philosophy

Each agent's system prompt is crafted from three layers:

1. **Strategic Doctrine** — What game theory strategy does this nation follow? (tit-for-tat, grim trigger, Pavlov, chicken)
2. **Real-World Context** — Actual alliances, dependencies, rivalries, and historical grievances
3. **Visceral Voice** — The agent speaks in-character, making decisions feel authentic rather than abstract

The result is agents that don't just play game theory — they embody the psychological, historical, and cultural factors that drive real geopolitical decisions.
